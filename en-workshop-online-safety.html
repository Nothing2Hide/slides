URLs

Lear how to read an url to avoid phishing type attacks:

protocol://subdomain.domain.tld

Who does this address belong to? https://www.facebook.secure.com/friends/

Interception Points

http S ?

What the S from https stands for?

  1. Safran, the name of the company that created httpsS
  2. Secure
  3. S as the plural of httpS

http S ?

  1. Authenticity: https certificates = site ID
  2. Privacy: traffic to the site is encrypted
  3. Integrity: data sent cannot be altered

Solution

  1. Use Edge Chrome or Firefox
  2. Install Duck Duck Go Privacy Essentials on Firefox or Chrome
  3. Use the Duck Duck Privacy Browser on Android or iOS

Problem

Many (80%) sites offer a secure version but not all. The https protocol secures data exchanges between you and the site you visit but it does not hide the domain names.

Your ISP, employer, preferred subway shop, etc. knows where you go online.

In France The duration of logs retention is 1 year since 2006 and the LCEN

Solution

Use a VPN

A VPN protects and encrypts your connection.

Online traces

If your traffic is encrypted, that doesn't mean you're anonymous. Beside our IP address, we leave a huge amount of online traces: OS version, browser version, language used, plugins isntalled, screen width, etc. This is our digital fingerprint. It can be used to trace us.

Check on AmIUnique.org how unique your fingerprint is.

Solution

Use Tor

Tor not only secure your traffic but also changes your digital fingerprint. Tor is the perfect tool when it comes to anonymity.

Tor or a VPN doesn't make you anonymous every time. Example: logging into your facebook account firstname.lastname with a VPN or Tor is breaking your anonymity.

To go even further

Use Tails, a portable operating system that protects against surveillance and censorship.
