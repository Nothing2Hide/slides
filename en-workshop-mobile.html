Turned off

The battery, plugged in, is still a source of energy. With the collaboration of the telecom operator, which can access the packets sent to the SIM card (Application Protocol Data Unit), the phone can theoretically be activated.

Battery removed

Without a battery, there is no power, no transmitted signals, no monitoring.

Data everywhere

  • On the sim card
  • In the phone memory
  • On the SD card
  • In the "cloud"

Data

  • Protect your Android or iOS smartphone with a password (more than 20 characters or delete after 10 attempts)
  • Encrypt the content of your smartphone running Android or iOS
In iOS the content is automatically encrypted (since iOS8), from the moment you set a pin code. Same for Android since Android 6. Do not forget the data on external memories (SD cards, sim card) and possibly stored in the cloud (automatically encrypted under ios but not necessarily with other services).

Tella: a local safe

Tella is an android app that allows you to take pictures and videos and to store them encrypted, to hide them, or even erase them in case of emergency.

Application rights and permissions

Just like on a laptop or a desktop computer, you must not install just any application on your smartphone. You have to check the rights that an application claims. Is it normal that a flashlight application needs to access to your address book?

There are 2 major application ecosystems today: Android and Apple.

  1. Apple is known for its controls and validations before any public release.
  2. Android (Google), well not so much.

Depending on your Android version(6 and above), you will need to search most often in Settings > Applications > ( sometimes Advanced Settings) > Apps permission.

In order to check the permissions of a spceific Android app, have a look at the Exodus Privacy project.
